## 🎯 Project Overview

**World Wine Explorer** is a full-stack analytics project that investigates the relationship between geography, weather, grape variety, and wine quality. Starting from raw data ingestion through to a polished, interactive dashboard, the project combines data engineering, SQL modelling, and business intelligence to answer real questions about what makes a great wine.

The dataset combines wine ratings and pricing data with geographic and climatic growing conditions — allowing us to explore how the *terroir* (soil, climate, region) shapes the wines we drink and the prices we pay for them.

---

## 🔍 Key Questions We Answered

- **How do geographic conditions affect wine quality?** Does latitude, altitude, or region consistently predict higher ratings?
- **What is the impact of climate and weather on wine ratings?** How do temperature, rainfall, and sunshine hours during the growing season correlate with scores?
- **Which grape varieties produce the highest-rated wines by region?** Are certain varietals better suited to specific climates?
- **What is the relationship between price and rating?** Does spending more guarantee a better wine?
- **Where are the best-value wines in the world?** High ratings, lower prices — by country and variety.
- **How do growing conditions translate into flavour profiles and commercial success?** Connecting the earth to the glass.

## 📦 Dataset

**Source:** [Wine Growth Weather — Kaggle](https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/abcd334/wine-growth-weather/data)

> *"Heaven, Earth, and Humanity: How Weather Affects the Flavor and Quality of Wine"*

The dataset covers wine production regions worldwide, combining:
- Wine ratings and critic scores
- Pricing data by variety and region
- Geographic data (country, region, latitude/longitude)
- Weather and climate variables during growing seasons (temperature, rainfall, sunshine hours)
- Grape variety classifications
